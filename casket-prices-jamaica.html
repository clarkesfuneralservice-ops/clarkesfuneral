Frequently Asked Questions About Casket Prices

What is the cheapest casket available in Jamaica?

The most affordable caskets are cremation caskets ($75,000-$95,000) suitable for cremation services with viewing. For traditional burial, basic wood caskets ($125,000-$145,000) made of pine or cedar with natural or painted finish are the most budget-friendly option. These provide dignity and quality at an affordable price.

What's the difference between wood and metal caskets?

Wood caskets are traditional, warm in appearance, and available in various finishes. They range from $125,000 (basic wood) to $350,000 (premium wood). Metal caskets ($450,000-$500,000) offer protective sealing and are more modern in style. Both are suitable for burial. The choice often comes down to personal preference, budget, and desired level of protection.

Does the casket price include the funeral service?

No. Casket prices listed here are for the casket only. A complete funeral service includes the casket PLUS funeral director services, embalming, transportation, programs, and more. Our complete burial packages range from $310,000 to $825,000. View complete funeral packages.

Can I bring my own casket?

Yes, you can purchase a casket from any provider and we will use it for the funeral service. However, we recommend reviewing the casket in person to ensure it meets your expectations. Our selection offers quality options at competitive prices with the convenience of seeing before purchasing.

What casket is best for cremation?

For cremation, we recommend a cremation casket ($75,000-$95,000) or a basic wood casket ($125,000-$145,000). These are suitable for viewing and cremation. Metal caskets cannot be cremated. See our cremation packages for complete pricing.

Do you offer payment plans for caskets?

Yes, we offer flexible payment plans to help families afford the casket and funeral services they want. We understand that funeral costs can be challenging and we're here to work with your budget. Call us at 876-326-1263 to discuss payment options.

How do I choose the right casket?

Consider: (1) Your budget, (2) Material preference (wood vs. metal), (3) Whether it's for burial or cremation, (4) Your loved one's preferences or personality, (5) Religious or cultural requirements. Our team can show you options in person and help you make the right choice without pressure.

Are imported caskets worth the extra cost?

Imported premium caskets ($550,000-$650,000) offer superior materials like bronze or copper, ornate designs, and luxury finishes. However, our locally-made standard wood caskets ($165,000-$185,000) provide excellent quality and beauty at a fraction of the cost. Most families choose local options for the best value. Premium wood caskets ($280,000-$350,000) offer an excellent middle-ground option.

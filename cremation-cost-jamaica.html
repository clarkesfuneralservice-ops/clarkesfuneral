Frequently Asked Questions About Cremation Cost in Jamaica

What is the cheapest cremation option in Jamaica?

The most affordable cremation option is Direct Cremation at $190,000. This includes all essential services: transfer, refrigeration, funeral director services, death registration, cremation fee, and delivery of ashes in a temporary container.

How long does the cremation process take in Jamaica?

The cremation process typically takes 3-5 days from the time of death to receiving the ashes. This includes obtaining necessary permits, paperwork, and the cremation itself. We handle all documentation to make the process as smooth as possible.

Can I have a viewing before cremation?

Yes! Our Cremation + Viewing package ($240,000) includes embalming, professional preparation, and a private viewing at our Kingston facility. This allows family members to say their final goodbyes before cremation.

What's included in the cremation fee?

The cremation fee covers the actual cremation process at the crematory, use of the cremation chamber, and basic urn or temporary container. Additional memorial items like decorative urns can be purchased separately.

Is cremation accepted by all religions in Jamaica?

Cremation is accepted by most Christian denominations in Jamaica, including Anglican, Methodist, and Presbyterian churches. Some denominations prefer traditional burial. We work respectfully with all religious traditions and can guide you through the decision.

What happens to the ashes after cremation?

After cremation, the ashes (cremated remains) are placed in a temporary container or urn of your choice. Families can keep the ashes at home, scatter them in a meaningful location, or place them in a columbarium. We provide guidance on all options.
